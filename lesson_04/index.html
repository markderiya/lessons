London is the capital of Great Britain

London is one of the largest cities and ports in the world. It is the British capital, the centre of the United Kingdom. London arose from a number of towns and villages which formed one urban area of 1,800 square km with a population of more than 8 million people.
London is situated on the River Thames about forty miles from its mouth. It is divided into two unequal portions by the river, the more important part stands on the north bank.
The population of London is probably the most variable in the world: the English, French, Italians, Russians, Poles, Swiss, Indians, Germans, etc.
The oldest part of London is the City, where all the business and financial life is concentrated on an area of only a little more than one square mile. It is about 2,000 years old.
Many monuments of London’s history can be found in the City. Its narrow streets are lined with buildings which house the offices and banks, insurance companies and trading companies. One street in this area is busy day and night. This is Fleet Street, in and round which nearly all important newspapers and press agencies have their offices.
The West End is the most beautiful residential area of London. There are lovely parks, luxurious mansions and elegant stores and restaurants, museums and hotels in the West End. The Queen lives in Buckingham Palace nowadays, and the Palace of Westminster is now the seat of the Parliament.
One of the busiest places in London is Trafalgar Square with Nelson’s column in it. Piccadilly is also the busiest spot, the Heart of London.
The East End of London is as different from the West End as can be. The East End is the area where the poor have their home. Their life and work concentrates round the Port of London.
London is one of the biggest industrial centres of Great Britain. It gives 1/6 of manufacturing industry production. Among many important industries we can name the following: machine-building industry, paper and printing and publishing industry, food-stuff industry, sewing industry, etc.
London is one of the biggest and most important transport centres of the country with its sea ports, airports, etc.
